# Flywheel Goal

Mission Anchor

Flywheel is ZestStream's meta-substrate: the operating system that turns worker outcomes, cross-orch fuckup rows, Jeff-pattern mining, and reusable skills into better future work. Its business job is to raise substrate_compounding_rate and skill quality so portfolio subs and service engagements receive compounding capability, not one-off automation.

Scope Clarifier

This is a standing operating system goal: Flywheel keeps running as substrate. It is not a delivery goal and it does not close when a public repo looks ready. Publishability is deferred to on or after H1 day-60, about 2026-07-17, if the substrate-readiness rubric passes; any public release ships through a separate delivery goal.

End-to-End Loop

1. Ingest worker callbacks, br events, dispatch-log.jsonl, fuckup-log.jsonl, SkillOS handoffs, and Jeff corpus updates.
2. Classify each input as substrate-compounding signal, Jeff-Pattern Quality Anchor coverage, flywheel-as-meta-substrate reuse, or blocker.
3. Route work into beads, skill hardening, doctrine repair, and cross-orch handoffs when SkillOS owns the capability surface.
4. Measure monthly substrate_compounding_rate, SkillOS skill_quality_bar_coverage_ratio, and evidence that Flywheel primitives improved another repo or pane.
5. Feed the learning into the next dispatch packet as gates, reservations, probes, receipts, or blocker classes, so failures become beads or fuckup rows.
