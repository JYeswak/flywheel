What I do

The software is not the villain. The missing route is.

Most companies are not short on software. They are short on a clear path from "customer asked" to "the right person did the next thing." That is why owners stay stretched even after buying the tools.

01 Customer asksThe request lands somewhere informal.

02 Team checksThe answer lives in pieces.

03 Someone chasesThe next step depends on memory.

04 Time leaksSaturdays become catch-up time.

The cost shows up as stress before it shows up as a line item.

05 Trust bendsA customer feels the miss.

How I work Start with the operating route.

I ask what starts the work, who touches it, where it stalls, what should stop it cold, and what a useful first win would save. That is the same pattern I used to cut my own 40-hour role to 15.

The Yuzu Method ™ Peel. Press. Pour.™

Peel is the discovery week. Press is the build. Pour is launch plus continuous improvement. The names matter less than the behavior: see the work, ship the fix, keep what proves useful.
