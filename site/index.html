Your business already has the data. The work is just hidden between tools.

Email, CRM, calendars, invoices, documents, and reports all hold part of the truth. The costly work is the copying, checking, chasing, and remembering between them.

The real SMB problem

The mess has a shape.

Owners are not wrong to be cautious about AI. The useful work starts by naming the route people already walk every day: the handoffs, re-entry, screenshots, exceptions, and owner-memory decisions.

01 RequestSomeone asks for help in email.

The request is real, but it is not yet work the system can follow.

02 StatusThe CRM knows part of the story.

The team checks it because the next step may not be obvious.

03 ScheduleThe date changes somewhere else.

The follow-up work moves, but not every tool hears it.

04 MoneyPayment or invoice state creates the next task.

Someone still has to notice, decide, and update the place people trust.

05 MemoryThe owner fills the gaps.

That is expensive work, even when nobody calls it a workflow.

One slice first

A slice is one bounded workflow improvement: useful enough to feel, small enough to inspect, and clear enough to stop.

A first slice might route an email request into the right follow-up list, update the place the owner already checks when an appointment changes, or create the checklist item the team would have made by hand.

Before

Five tools hold five versions of the same job, so people copy, check, and chase.

Slice

One route gets mapped, built, reviewed, and measured without pretending the whole business is automated.

Proof

The result is passed, blocked, private, or skipped with a reason. No claim moves forward just because it sounds good.
