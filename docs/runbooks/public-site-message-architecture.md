# Public Site Message Architecture — Operator Spine

Decided 2026-05-14 by Joshua. Every prior rewrite of the public site was
rejected because it had no spine — it sold the *method* (Yuzu / Flywheel /
proof-path / trust-controls, described four times in four treatments) and
never sold the *outcome* or *why Joshua*.

The strategic constraint: Flywheel is pre-customer (the "internal proof first"
decision), and clients cannot be named without per-surface consent. So the
proof that carries "why work with me" is **Joshua's operating background** —
not a client outcome, not the method.

## The rule every section obeys

Every section must do a **persuasive job** that moves an SMB owner toward
hiring Joshua. A section that only *describes* — the method, the principles,
the repo — is filler. Cut it. The grammatical subject of the copy is the
**owner** and **Joshua**. Never the page, the story, the method, or the repo.

## The spine — section order and the job each section does

1. **Hero — who Joshua is, and what it is costing them.**
   The first sentence establishes Joshua as an operator: 12 years running
   operations, MBA, ran ZIRKEL — not an AI vendor. Then the owner's real
   stake — not "work trapped between tools" (abstract) but the felt cost:
   working Saturdays, can't step away from the business, a job fell through a
   crack and cost a customer. Job: in three seconds the owner knows who this
   is and that he understands their problem.

2. **The turn — it is not your software, it is the handoffs.**
   "You already bought the tools. The expensive part is the copy, check,
   chase, and remember work *between* them. That is an operations problem —
   and operations problems are what I fixed for 12 years." Job: bridge
   Joshua's background to their pain.

3. **Why me — the differentiator.** (NEW — currently missing entirely.)
   Joshua versus the typical AI consultant: most people selling AI have never
   run a P&L; Joshua has. He does not start with a model — he triages the
   route that is eating your week, the way an operator does. The Yuzu Method
   (Peel / Press / Pour) appears *here*, as evidence of how an operator works
   — not as a branded framework to admire. Job: answer "why you, not the
   other guy."

4. **What you get — the outcome.**
   Concrete and outcome-framed, not process-framed: in weeks, not a
   transformation project — one route mapped, one slice fixed, a clear
   before/after of what it saved. "You keep the operating judgment; I wire the
   system." Job: make the result tangible.

5. **Why you can trust it.**
   Fold the old "trust controls" here, reframed per-fear: the owner's actual
   worry, then why it does not apply to working with Joshua. "Blocked is
   better than bluffing" is the trust anchor. The public-git-history idea
   collapses to ONE line here — not its own room. Job: dissolve the risk of
   hiring an AI person.

6. **The offer and next step.**
   Keep — it is close. "Send me one workflow that keeps getting copied or
   chased. Redacted is fine. That is the whole first step." Job: a
   low-commitment, concrete next action.

## Cuts

- **"A real slice, from this repo" — CUT entirely.** The website talking about
  its own quality gate. The purest "words on the page to put words on the
  page." No replacement until a real *client* slice exists to tell.
- **"Proof path" (Map / Block / Verify / Reuse) — CUT or fold.** The method
  described a fourth time. The one usable idea — git history survives
  inspection — becomes a single line in section 5.
- **"For reviewers" — SHRINK.** Honestly segmented for technical evaluators,
  low harm, but it must not be a full section competing with the owner's
  spine. Minimal block near the footer.
- **Hero "scene" (Email / CRM / Calendar / Invoice nodes) — REDESIGN.** It is
  method-theater, and it has a layout bug: the absolutely-positioned nodes
  overlap the hero text on wide viewports ("words on words"). Either replace
  it with a cleaner visual that supports the operator framing, or properly
  constrain it to a bounded grid that never collides with text at any width.

## Gate follow-up (not now)

FQ-11 catches "subject = the page." It does NOT catch "subject = the method,
on loop" or "section with no persuasive job" — which is why the page scored
12/0/0 and was still rejected. That is an FQ-15 to add *after* this page is
fixed and can serve as the calibration fixture — not before.
